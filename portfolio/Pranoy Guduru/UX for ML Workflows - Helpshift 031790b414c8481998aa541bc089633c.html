UX for ML Workflows - Helpshift

My Role & Contribution

I worked as a designer with the AI-triad (has an specialist each in Product, Engg, Design) team at Helpshift. The team was responsible to evaluate needs of customers, design and deliver the ML features for the product.

Responsibilities & Deliverables

  • Understand and evaluate needs of the customers. Collaborate with PM & Engineering.
  • Ideate AI workflows for the product.
  • Deliver UX flows and wireframes.
  • Testing, synthesising insights and improving the UX for all the personas.
  • Designing on-boarding guides.

Impact

  • I worked on the product area for 3 major milestones for the product area. From opening up the MVP to all customers till advanced features like segmentation & personalisation.
  • Working on the ML Features in phases and taking constant user feedback to improve.

Phase 1. Opening the feature to all existing customers

Bringing focus to the training workflow & Basic hygiene

Onboarding guide with Pendo

  • The ML features which were in an MVP, were opening up to all the existing customers. We designed an on-boarding guide with Pendo (a 3rd party on-boarding plugin) that helps admins discover/understand the new features, eases them into its workflow and explore possibilities with it.
  • Communicate —> Guide has 4 chapters (Creating intent groups & intents, Training intents, Creating an intent menu, adding intent menu to an app) that provides support at every job.

Phase 2. Training message suggestions

Customers were adding training samples, but not sufficient number or for all the intents.

The system could suggest training samples based on one provided by the user.

Phase 3. Segmentation and personalisation

Customers wanted to show certain options only to certain segments of their users. Example, give the Talk to specialist to their VIP users.

Challenge 1 : Segmentation adds 2 more steps to configure the experience. This advanced feature (ask from already using customers) could make it difficult customers starting to use it.

Design decision : Hide adding segments inside a click.

Challenge 2 : UX is fragmented. There are 3 different parts of the platform (Configuring user segments, configuring intent menus & Configuring app settings) to configure the experience. This could cause a lot of confusion.

  • Provide context with popovers, jump to page on clicking on the popover.
