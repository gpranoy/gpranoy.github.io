Creative Writing

This is a story I wrote for one of the challenges. It serves as a subtext or “Why” are we doing this activity..

Chapter 3, Intro to wacky inventions

It was a no-moon night and I heard that the stars were to have a party! I stepped out with my bicycle. I cycled down the street. To look at the stars, I looked up at the sky. But, There was a huge board blocking my view. I cycled ahead. There was another one bigger than the previous one. Ironically, This one had blinding lights. You were to see only it and nothing else when around it. A zombie was hypnotized by it. It was drooling, looking at the things on the board. The board had a drawing of a huge bucket and said that the zombie had to have it. I took a turn on the next junction. The whole street, wherever my eyes wanted to rest was kicked by a board with blinding lights with bucket drawings.

There was a huge mob of zombies in front of a multi-mega-super-hyper-bucket store blocking the whole street. The zombies already had multiple buckets on their heads and if you ask me, they were fine. This new bucket, the one on the boards was nothing new. It was the same thing. But, the zombies were already hypnotized. They had no escape. Wherever they could look, they would see the bucket drawings. On the road, on their mobile screens, in their films, in the news and even in their dreams. I learnt that evening that they were not deprived of dreaming, The boards hijacked them. The zombies in front of the shop wouldn’t stop at anything, fight their way for the bucket. The vehicles on the street were in a fix. I could escape and I made my way into a small lane where there were no lights but my bicycle’s dynamo. I was relieved. I could finally choose what to see. I wanted to look at the stars but the smoke from the cars put everything out of sight.

I followed a zombie to see what those buckets were for. Apparently, they don’t like other shapes than the ones their buckets could hold. They would beat down glorious shapes and fit them in their buckets.

It is observed that participating in mindless consumption, humans were becoming zombies. The products were designed for easy consumption and lauded their laziness. Their designs were working, many were losing their ability to imagine and create.

To stand-up and revolt against this, Today we’ll come up with the most wackiest inventions we could dream up!

Adapting for Classroom.

I would write down the main ideas in the form of a story/narrative. This we’d adapt for a classroom setting.

For example, I wrote this for the introductory classes.

We were all superheroes. We could parkour, dance all night, build bridges across seas. A spider didn’t have to bite me so that I could become someone special. I didn’t have to be born in a certain community or in an alien land. As I said, we were all superheroes. We could make things that could let us swim with the mighty blue whales, swing a few satellites onto passing comets.

The main idea was to tell kids that Creative abilities are human superpowers. Give them the confidence to try out..

This one we made a Guessing Game, where kids would be asked about superheroes who could swim with whales or go as fast as sound. The answers were generally around fictional characters, but the punchline for the story drops it as human creative ability.

Maker Faire Hyderabad. 2018

Better By Design designed and executed the Kids Experience for MakerFaire Hyderabad, 2018. My contributions to the project included in the concept design and strategy for the exhibitions, events and workshops.

Alivers’ Metropolis

The exhibition space was to evoke and transport kids and parents to a world full of ideas. Extending the world of Alivers, We chose the metaphor of Alivers Metropolis. A city built by and for Alivers. The space showcased kids’ projects from Telangana and Andhra Pradesh. It had spaces where kids could get a peek into to aspects of design, creative thinking and making through activities and workshops.
